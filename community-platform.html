🏛️SBA Policy

SBA SOP 50 10 8 — What Changed and Why It Matters

Plain language: The SBA's Standard Operating Procedure governs how all SBA loans are processed. Recent updates expanded eligibility for businesses in underserved communities and simplified the application process for loans under $500K.

What this means for you: If you were previously told you didn't qualify for SBA financing, it's worth re-applying under the updated rules. Community-focused businesses have broader access than before.

🌆Local Policy

California AB 1557 — Small Business Support Act

Plain language: This bill expands access to state-backed loan guarantees for businesses in low-income census tracts. It works alongside federal SBA programs to reduce collateral requirements.

What this means for you: Business owners in qualifying areas may be able to secure financing with less collateral. Contact AmPac to see if your business location qualifies.

👷Workforce Policy

WIOA — Workforce Innovation & Opportunity Act Explained

Plain language: WIOA is the federal law that funds job training programs in your community. It's why organizations like Uplift Workforce Alliance can offer free training — it's federally subsidized through this act.

What this means for you: If you're a nonprofit offering workforce services, you may be eligible for WIOA funding. If you need job training, WIOA-funded programs are free to eligible participants.

🏘️Community Dev

New Markets Tax Credit (NMTC) Program — Plain Language Guide

Plain language: NMTC allows investors to receive a 39% federal tax credit over 7 years for investing in low-income community businesses. This makes financing in underserved areas more attractive to private capital.

What this means for you: If you're developing a commercial project in a qualifying area, NMTC-backed financing could significantly reduce your cost of capital.
